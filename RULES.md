# Scoring Rules

## Zone Scoring

Each hole has scoring zones arranged vertically from tee to green. The ball (drink level) lands in a zone, and that zone determines the score for the hole.

| Zone | Score | Position |
|------|-------|----------|
| +5   | Worst | Farthest from green (near tee) |
| +4   |       | |
| +3   |       | |
| +2   |       | |
| +1   |       | |
| 0    |       | Just above green |
| -1   | Best  | On the green |
| +1   |       | Just past green |
| +2   |       | Over next tee area |

**Lower score = better.** Landing on the green (-1) is the best outcome.

## Above-Green Zones (+5 through 0)

Zones narrow as they approach the green:
- +5 is the widest zone (most forgiving for bad shots)
- 0 is the thinnest zone (hardest to land in, just above the green)

Zone widths are adjusted by hole difficulty and par:
- Harder holes (low handicap) get slightly wider zones (more forgiving)
- Par 3s have tighter zones; par 5s are more forgiving

## Below-Green Zones (+1, +2)

If the ball goes past the green:
- **+1**: The gap between the current green and the next hole's tee box
- **+2**: Over the next hole's tee box area

The next hole's +5 zone starts immediately after the +2 zone (hard cutoff).

## Tee Box Rule

If you start on or before a tee box and don't even make it to your own hole's +5 zone, it counts as **+5**. This is the equivalent of not making it past the forward tee — you didn't even get the ball in play.

## Boundary Between Holes

- Each hole's scoring zones tile perfectly with no gaps
- The previous hole's below-green zones (+1, +2) extend into the next hole's tee area
- The next hole's +5 zone begins immediately after the previous hole's +2 ends
- There is no neutral or unscored space between holes
